Cranberry No-Knead Bread

Dried cranberries in a slow-fermented loaf.

← Back to Recipes
Prep Time 5 minutes
Rise Time 12-18 hours
Bake Time 40-45 min
Yield 1 loaf

This no-knead cranberry bread relies on time and restraint to create balance. A long, slow fermentation develops structure and depth while dried cranberries soften naturally in the dough, adding gentle sweetness without overwhelming the crumb. The result is a loaf that is tender, lightly complex, and well-suited for both everyday slicing and quiet occasions.

Baker's Note This loaf continues to surprise me.

The crumb is soft and moist, the crust gently crisp, and the cranberries bring just enough brightness without overpowering the bread itself. There's a quiet sweetness here, not dessert sweet, just warm and balanced.

What I notice most, though, is what happens when people taste it. Smiles. A pause. That small moment where conversation slows and everyone takes another bite.

That's the kind of bread I want to make, simple, comforting, and quietly joyful.

Ingredients

  • 3¾ cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • ¾ cup dried cranberries
  • 1¼ cups cool water

Method

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, salt, yeast, and dried cranberries until evenly distributed.
  2. Add the water and stir just until no dry flour remains. The dough will be sticky and uneven.
  3. Cover the bowl and let the dough rise at room temperature for 12–18 hours, until it has expanded and shows bubbles on the surface.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Fold it over itself once or twice to form a rough round.
  5. Cover loosely and let rest for 30 minutes while the oven preheats.
  6. Preheat the oven to 450°F with a Dutch oven inside.
  7. Carefully place the dough into the hot Dutch oven, cover with the lid, and bake for 30 minutes.
  8. Remove the lid and bake an additional 10–15 minutes, until the crust is deep golden and firm.
  9. Transfer to a rack and allow to cool completely before slicing.

The finished loaf. Deeply golden crust with visible cranberries throughout.

The Crumb

A well-developed crumb with evenly distributed cranberries and an open, tender structure.

Notes / Variations

Variation. Cranberry Beer Bread

For a deeper, more developed flavor, replace ¾ to 1 cup of the water with beer. Use a neutral or lightly malty beer and avoid highly bitter styles. This version produces a softer crumb and more pronounced character while maintaining the same fermentation and bake process.

A small amount of honey (1–2 tablespoons) and a teaspoon of vinegar may be added to balance sweetness and acidity. These additions are optional and reflect earlier test versions of this loaf.
