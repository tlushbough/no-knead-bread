Cheddar No-Knead Bread

Pockets of medium cheddar in a slow-fermented loaf.

← Back to Recipes
Prep Time 5 minutes
Rise Time 12-18 hours
Bake Time 40-45 min
Yield 1 loaf

This cheddar loaf follows the same slow, patient process as our core no-knead bread, with one thoughtful addition. Cubes of medium cheddar are folded into the dough and allowed to melt naturally during the long rise and bake. The result is a quiet, reliable loaf with crisp crust, open crumb, and pockets of cheese throughout — nothing more, nothing less.

A Variation on the Core Method This is the first add-in recipe on the site. Small, thoughtful changes create a new loaf without complicating the process. The cheese is folded into the dough, not layered or topped, so the loaf bakes cleanly with an intact crust and even oven spring.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1½ teaspoons kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon yeast (instant or active dry)
  • 1¼ cups cool water
  • 4 to 5 ounces medium cheddar cheese, cut into small cubes
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper (optional)

About the Cheese

Use six small cheddar snack blocks (each about 0.75 oz). Unwrap all six and cut them into small cubes roughly the size of a pea to a marble. Do not shred the cheese. Do not reserve any for topping. All cheese goes inside the dough.

Why Cubed Cheese?

Cubed cheese melts into distinct pockets during baking, preserving the structure of the loaf and keeping the crumb open, unlike shredded cheese which tends to disappear into the dough.

Method

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, yeast, and optional pepper.
  2. Add the water and stir until a shaggy, sticky dough forms. There should be no dry flour.
  3. Gently fold in all of the cubed cheese. Do not overmix.
  4. Cover the bowl loosely and let the dough rise at room temperature for 12 to 18 hours, until doubled and bubbly.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Fold once or twice and shape into a loose round. Do not knead.
  6. Let the dough rest for 20 to 30 minutes while the oven heats.
  7. Preheat the oven to 450°F with a covered Dutch oven inside for 30 minutes.
  8. Transfer the dough to parchment paper and carefully place it into the hot Dutch oven.
  9. Bake 30 minutes with the lid on.
  10. Remove the lid and bake an additional 10 to 15 minutes, until deeply golden.
  11. Remove from the oven and let cool for at least 30 minutes before slicing.

Key Guidance

  • The dough should look rough and uneven before rising. This is correct.
  • Do not add extra flour after mixing.
  • Do not knead.
  • Cubed cheese is intentional and should not be replaced with shredded cheese.
  • All cheese is incorporated into the dough, not on the surface.
